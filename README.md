# Houston Rockets Promotional Messaging Strategy

## Project Overview
This project analyzes Houston Rockets fan engagement and customer behavior data from the 2022–23 NBA season to develop a targeted promotional messaging strategy for the 2023–24 season opener. The objective was to help the marketing team optimize a limited quota of 500 digital messages by identifying high-value customer segments and recommending efficient allocation strategies.

The analysis focuses on:
- Fan attendance behavior
- Spending patterns
- Demographic segmentation
- Digital channel engagement
- Marketing allocation strategy

The final deliverable included a two-slide recommendation deck summarizing insights and actionable recommendations for the Houston Rockets marketing department.

---

# Project Goals

The primary goals of this project were to:

1. Analyze customer and engagement data to identify trends
2. Determine the most valuable fan segments
3. Recommend how to allocate 500 promotional messages effectively
4. Improve fan engagement for the season opener
5. Support long-term fanbase growth through targeted outreach

---

# Business Questions Addressed

The project answered several key business questions:

- Which customer segments generate the highest revenue?
- Which fan groups show the strongest attendance patterns?
- Which demographics offer the greatest growth opportunity?
- What digital platforms should be prioritized for outreach?
- How should the marketing team distribute 500 digital messages?

---

# Key Insights

## High-Value Customer Segments
The analysis identified the most profitable and engaged fans as:

- Fans attending 10+ games
- Fans earning $250,000+ annually
- Fans aged 45–54
- Fans located in ZIP codes:
  - 77461
  - 77565
  - 77498

These groups demonstrated significantly higher spending and attendance behavior. Fans attending 10+ games spent approximately $1,000 more than the average fan.

---

## Growth Opportunity Segments
The project also identified under-engaged audiences with strong long-term growth potential:

- Fans attending 10 or fewer games
- Fans under age 35
- Fans with incomes below $100,000
- Families with 3+ children
- Fans with high school education or equivalent
- Fans in ZIP codes:
  - 77069
  - 77030
  - 77532
  - 77036

These groups represent opportunities for future fan development and increased engagement.

---

# Messaging Allocation Strategy

## Loyal / High-Spending Fans
**70% of total messages (350 messages)** were allocated toward:
- Fans attending 10+ games
- High-income fans
- Core attendance demographics

This strategy prioritized maximizing immediate revenue and engagement from already loyal customers.

---

## Emerging / Growth Fans
**30% of total messages (150 messages)** were allocated toward:
- Lower-attendance fans
- Younger demographics
- Lower-income audiences

The objective was to build long-term fan loyalty and expand the Rockets fanbase.

---

# Digital Channel Analysis

The project evaluated digital platform engagement among fans and recommended prioritizing:

1. Twitter (X)
2. Facebook

These platforms showed the highest user engagement:
- Twitter followers: 1542
- Facebook followers: 1541
- Instagram followers: 1498
- Mobile app users: 1451

The average fan engaged with approximately two digital platforms.
